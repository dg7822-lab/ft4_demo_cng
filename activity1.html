Frequent Power Cuts in Village Schools

Problem: Many village schools face power cuts during classes, affecting lighting and computer education. These interruptions disrupt lessons and make it difficult for teachers to conduct digital or practical sessions. Without consistent electricity, students cannot use projectors, fans, or computers efficiently, which limits their learning experience.

Simple Idea: Build a small hybrid system using wind + biogas to provide continuous power. The wind turbine can supply electricity during the day, while the biogas generator can run when thereâ€™s little or no wind. This system uses local renewable sources and is sustainable, affordable, and environment-friendly.

Advantages:

  • Reduces dependence on fossil fuels and promotes green energy.
  • Provides continuous electricity to rural schools and small community centers.
  • Encourages local waste management through biogas use from organic waste.
  • Reduces carbon emissions and long-term operational costs.
  • Improves learning opportunities by ensuring stable power for digital education.
  • Can be scaled up to serve nearby homes or health centers.
  • Easy to maintain using locally available materials and resources.
