Lack of Freshwater in Coastal Villages

Problem: Many coastal villages face acute freshwater shortages due to overexploited groundwater and rising salinity caused by seawater intrusion. Traditional water supply through tankers or pipelines is costly, unreliable, and unsustainable in the long term. During dry seasons, communities often travel long distances just to access drinkable water, impacting both health and productivity.

Simple Idea: Develop a small-scale desalination system powered entirely by wave energy. The natural motion of sea waves drives a mechanical or hydraulic pump that pressurizes seawater through a filtration or reverse osmosis system. This setup converts saline water into clean, drinkable water without relying on external electricity or fuel.

This innovative solution can be installed close to shorelines and customized for local needs. Since wave energy is available almost continuously, it ensures a reliable source of freshwater while minimizing carbon emissions and operational costs. The system can also include a small storage tank and UV purification for additional safety.
